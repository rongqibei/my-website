👋 Hello! I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the [University of Michigan School of Information](https://www.si.umich.edu/), advised by [Mark Newman](https://www.si.umich.edu/people/mark-newman) and [Pedja Klasnja](https://www.si.umich.edu/people/pedja-klasnja). I also work closely with [Noelle Carlozzi](https://medschool.umich.edu/profile/3716/noelle-e-carlozzi) at the [Center for Clinical Outcomes Development and Application](https://medresearch.umich.edu/labs-departments/centers/coda) in the University of Michigan Medical School. Prior to joining Michigan, I received my B.S. in Data Science from [Duke Kunshan University](https://www.dukekunshan.edu.cn/)/[Duke University](https://www.duke.edu/), where I worked with [Xin Tong](https://cma.hkust-gz.edu.cn/faculty-regular/tong-xin/).

My research sits at the intersection of **human-computer interaction (HCI)** and **digital health**. I design and evaluate interactive systems—from personalized mobile apps to LLM-powered conversational agents—that help people manage their well-being, with the goal of better aligning evolving technologies with people's quality of life needs. I care about building experiences that genuinely support people in their everyday lives, and much of my work applies field deployments and mixed methods to study these systems in high-stakes contexts.
